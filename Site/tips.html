Tips

Before you throw out the food, take a look at some of these tips.

Bananas

Got too many bananas? Don't worry, no need to force yourself to eat them all instead make some masks! Did you know the nutritional values in bananas help moisturize, add glow and have anti-aging benefits in them. Most importantly they help treat acne and the scars it can leave behind. Below is a common banana masks used for acne and scars.

Banana face mask for acne and scars:

  • 1/2 a banana
  • 1/2 tablespoon baking soda
  • 1/2 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
Application directions

Eggs

Are your eggs about to expire? Don't just throw them out or have omelettes for breakfast for a whole week. Eggs have been used in hair masks for the longest time and are known for helping keep your hair moisturized and strong. They can also help hair growth too! Below is an egg mask used for dull and frizzy hair.

Egg hair mask for dull and frizzy hair:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon virgin coconut oil
  • 1 tablespoon full-fat yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
Application directions

Potatoes

We often end up buying a whole sack of potatoes and end up using only a quarter of it and then wonder what to do with the rest. You'll be happy to know potatoes contain antioxidants and properties which help reduce spots and blemishes. Below is a mask used to brighten the skin:

Potato and turmeric face mask to brighten skin:

  • 1/2 a potato
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
